# DockWalk iOS WMS Deep Research Report

## 1. Executive product interpretation  
DockWalk is **first and foremost a true warehouse management system (WMS)** built for dockworkers and 3PL ops. It must support the *full cycle* of warehouse processes – **Inbound** (appointments, receiving, inspections, putaway, inventory) through **Outbound** (picking, staging, loading, shipping, returns) – on a single coherent mobile-friendly platform【18†L29-L38】【27†L83-L92】. Inbound and outbound each have scanner-first workflows with high error handling, while quality checks and cycle counts ensure data integrity. Critically, **DockWalk is not an enterprise SaaS “dashboard”**; it should feel built by a veteran dock operator for ten-hour shifts – fast, forgiving, and obvious. It **must support mobile store economics and real trucking payment flows, not consumer SaaS models**. Receiving organizations pay via App Store/Play IAP (app subscription), while drivers pay on the dock via plugged-in multi-POS (fleet cards, tap-to-pay, etc.)【19†L93-L100】【18†L34-L38】.  

**What DockWalk should be:** a field‑tested, mobile‑first WMS that makes a small/mid‑sized warehouse run like clockwork. It should *feel* practical, calibrated to everyday 3PL pain points (paperwork elimination, catching mispicks, minimizing detention fees). It must be simple enough to launch without six‑figure IT projects, yet modular so that advanced features (AI inspection, OCR, payment connectors) can slot in later.  

**What it should *not* be:** an abstract “AI camera” demo or a bloated desktop software. The MVP identity is *dock operations*, not AI or invoicing. Use the founder’s 12 years in 3PL to keep the product grounded: “solved by people who lived it.” The app’s positioning should underscore *affordable, practical WMS* for teams priced out of legacy software. (We’ll craft App Store copy and screenshots around speed, clarity, and “built by real dock workers” themes.)  

## 2. Complete inbound workflow  
The inbound workflow begins **before truck arrival** and ends when goods are put away in inventory. Key steps (with roles, data, screens, exceptions) include:

- **Appointment scheduling:** The warehouse or lumper admin creates a dock appointment (date/time, dock number, carrier, BOL info). _(User: Supervisor/admin; Action: set or confirm appointment; Data: dock ID, vehicle, carrier, expected PO/ASN; Screen: appointment board or mobile calendar.)_  
- **Advanced Ship Notice (ASN) / PO creation:** Incoming POs/ASNs are entered (manually or via API/CSV) so the WMS knows what to expect【27†L85-L94】. _(Data: PO number, item SKUs, quantities, vendor, BOL #; Screen: PO/ASN list; Exception: missing PO creates blind receiving flow.)_  
- **Driver check-in:** Upon arrival, the driver scans or confirms the appointment code at the dock. The app logs *arrival time*, gate info, and any early/late status. _(User: Driver or dock clerk; Action: scan QR on appointment or enter truck number; Data: appointment ID, actual arrival timestamp; Screen: “Driver check-in” with camera for QR; Status: Appointment → “Arrived”.)_  
- **Detention tracking:** If the truck waits, an automatic timer logs detention start. The team can note reasons (traffic, paperwork hold) to later handle billing. _(Exception: extended wait; Screen: detention timer UI or notes entry.)_  
- **Unloading inspection:** The driver (or helper) unloads cargo. At any point, the user can start an **inspection session** – capturing photos or notes of damage/issues as goods come off the truck【27†L117-L125】【18†L49-L53】. Each **damage capture** is stamped with time, location, and (later) optional AI analysis. _(Action: “Start unloading inspection” on app; Screen: camera interface with overlay prompts, “mark damage” mode; Data: images + metadata saved to `inspection` records.)_  
- **Manifest/packing slip scan:** Each pallet/carton is scanned. The app matches scan data (UPC/QR/plate numbers) to the expected ASN or PO. _(User: Receiver with device; Action: scan pallet/carton label or license plate; Data: scanned tag, matched PO line; Screen: scan dialog showing “OK” or “Not on PO” feedback.)_  
- **Quantity/condition verification:** The app displays the expected quantity for each SKU. The user confirms counts, and flags any **overage/shortage or damages**【27†L132-L141】. _(Exception: if counts differ, create a discrepancy record; Screen: count input and reason prompt.)_  
- **Quality inspection (receiving QC):** For high-value or fragile goods, user performs spot checks – e.g. opening cases, scanning individual items. The app logs any failed quality check with a note/photo. _(Screen: QC checklist overlay; Data: pass/fail flags.)_  
- **Document capture:** At the end of each shipment, the user can **scan entire documents** (BOL, invoice, packing slip). The OCR route stub (`/api/ocr-document`) can later process these, but at minimum images are attached to the receiving record. _(Screen: document scan with rectangle overlay; Data: uploaded images.)_  
- **Location assignment & putaway:** Once unloaded, workers move goods into staging or storage. The WMS suggests storage locations (or the supervisor assigns). Each movement is scanned: the app shows a list of scanned items (license plates) and prompts user to scan the destination bin/rack. _(Action: “Putaway mode” in app; Data: LP→location mapping; Screen: scanner & location selection; State: moves change inventory from “inbound” to “stored”.)_  
- **Transaction closeout:** The user marks the receiving as complete. The app finalizes the inbound shipment, updates inventory counts, and optionally prints a receipt. This triggers any further workflows (like notifying inventory managers or billing the carrier for damages). _(Screen: “Complete receiving”; Data: final status ‘received’.)_  

At each step, statuses update (e.g. `appointment.scheduled→arrived→in_progress→completed`; `shipment.receiving_started→receiving_complete`; `item.onhand` etc.). **Exceptions** (no PO found, missing ASN, wrong item, damage) open prompts to note resolution actions (reject, return, claim initiation). The **iOS screens** should be minimal: a main “Receive” tab that lists today’s appointments/shipments, drill-in to a shipment view with “Scan”, “Inspect”, “Finish” actions. Use large buttons (“Scan Pallet”, “Add Damage”, “Complete”) and instant visual feedback (green check for success, red X for mismatch) to guide workers. (Refer to [27] for practices like verifying seals and capturing discrepancies【27†L109-L118】【27†L132-L141】.)

## 3. Complete outbound workflow  
Outbound starts with customer orders and ends when the truck departs, updating inventory along the way.

- **Order import/creation:** Orders enter the system via API, CSV import, or manual entry. Each order lists SKUs and quantities (plus destination, carrier). _(User: Supervisor or integrated system; Data: OrderID, items, shipment type; Screen: Order list; Status: “released” for picking.)_  
- **Pick planning (wave/batch):** The system groups orders into pick waves or batches by zone, priority, or order age. _(This can initially be simple: one order at a time.)_  
- **Picking:** A worker uses a handheld device in “Pick” mode. The app shows the target item and quantity, and prompts to scan the item’s bin location and then the item barcode. Barcode scanning verifies correct item and decrements quantity. _(User: Picker; Action: scan location, then item; Data: order-line, actual scanned qty; Screen: “Pick at [location] – 5 each [SKU]”; ID badge maybe to attribute action.)_ Errors (wrong item, short pick) trigger Soft Alerts as per [31†L92-L100] (non-blocking pop-ups) and allow quick correction. Every pick updates the inventory and order lines in real-time.【25†L332-L340】  
- **Packing (basic):** After picking, items move to packing. The packer confirms that the correct items/quantities were picked. The app can show a summary of the order and photo or scan the package. Optionally integrate a scales or label printer via API (for now stub). _(Screen: Order summary & “Confirm Pack” button.)_ The system can automatically generate carrier labels/BOL from order data.  
- **Outbound staging:** Orders ready to ship are staged by dock. The app shows a **dock door assignment** screen: driver arrival times and which orders belong to each dock. Packers scan packed pallets onto docks. Status changes from “Picked” to “Staged”【25†L349-L354】.  
- **Load planning:** When trucks arrive for loading, the foreman or driver confirms the load. The app verifies each pallet on the truck via scanning (sometimes called “Load verification”). The user scans the license plate or bin on the dock before loading and then scans the truck’s BOL or RFID. The system ensures all scanned pallets belong to this outbound shipment.  
- **Exceptions:** Shortages (inventory mismatch), substitutions, or last-minute order changes pop up a prompt (e.g. “Item not found – substitute?”). The app logs reasons and notifies supervisors if needed. Returns or cancelled items can be processed back into inventory.  
- **Shipment closeout:** Once loading is done, the user marks the shipment as **shipped**. The app finalizes the shipment: sends confirmation to carrier, updates inventory permanently (and any in-flight orders), and optionally prints a Bill of Lading or collects signature/photo from driver. The app can also record the GPS/photo at load finish. Finally, it logs the ship-depart time【25†L349-L354】.  

Through this flow, statuses evolve (e.g. `order.released→picking→packed→staged→shipped`). Outbound shipments become confirmed inventory decrements. Returns are handled by creating a new inbound exception case. At each step, API contracts would reflect actions (e.g. POST `/api/outbound/pick-item`, `/api/outbound/confirm-shipment`, etc.).  

**Citations:** These steps align with standard WMS practices: a WMS automates receiving and putting away goods, tracking inventory, then picking, packing, and shipping orders【25†L312-L320】【25†L349-L354】. The warehouse shipping page confirms this full cycle (receiving → inventory → picking → shipping)【25†L312-L320】【25†L349-L354】. Workflows like ours (scan-to-verify, guided picks, load checking) follow industry best practices.

## 4. MVP feature cut  
Not all features ship at once. Here’s a trimmed roadmap for MVP vs. later:

- **Must-have (MVP):**  
  - **Inbound receiving & putaway:** appointment board, PO/ASN management, barcode scanning for POs, count verification, basic QC.  
  - **Inventory lookup & cycle count:** ability to scan or search locations and SKUs, perform simple cycle counts (suspend during offline).  
  - **Outbound picking + staging + shipping:** scanning-guided picking, basic packing confirmation, staging status, and closeout scanning.  
  - **Offline support:** local syncing, retry queues, and UI indicators when offline【29†L621-L624】.  
  - **User roles (basic):** admin (config), clerk (scan+receive/pick), and viewer.  
  - **Synchronization:** Supabase sync for all changes, RLS for tenant isolation.  
  - **Core API:** auth, facilities, docks, appointments, shipments, inventory movements, tasks.  

- **Strong beta:**  
  - **Cycle counts improvements:** scheduled tasks, variance reporting.  
  - **Returns/exceptions:** simple screen to mark returns and adjust inventory.  
  - **Basic reporting/history:** view logs of recent jobs, inventory changes.  
  - **Supervisor/admin web UI:** small web view to see appointment board and inventory overview.  

- **Later:**  
  - **Detailed analytics:** inventory reports, KPI dashboards.  
  - **Advanced pick methods:** wave picking, zone picking optimization.  
  - **Bulk putaway heuristics:** auto-assign storage by rules.  
  - **3PL billing:** invoicing module, driver portals.  

- **Stubs (do not build yet):**  
  - **AI Inspection routes:** stub endpoints for `/api/analyze-damage`, `/ocr-document`, `/inspection/compile-report`. (Plan only.)  
  - **Full POS integration:** scaffold for payment_providers/providers, but no live payments.  
  - **Premium features:** e.g. Kitting, slotting optimization, voice pick hints (nice but later).  

Always prioritize core mobile flows (scan, confirm, update). Avoid heavy integration (ERP/SQL connectors) at first – treat external data as imports/exports.

## 5. Dock worker iOS UX model  
**Navigation and layout:** A bottom-tab bar with: “Dashboard” (today’s tasks), “Receive”, “Ship”, “Inventory”, and “More”/settings. Keep one-handed reach in mind: bottom or middle screen actions are preferable【31†L142-L150】. The app should default to the **Receive** screen after login (the most common flow), but a “Scan” floating button (center bottom) should always activate the camera for flexible scanning.【31†L69-L77】

**Scanner-first flows:** Scanning should be central. Use large central buttons or auto-focus camera on startup. Toggle the device’s camera scanner only when needed – e.g. disable scanning when an overlay dialog is open【31†L69-L77】. Provide instant feedback via sound and haptics: a quick “beep” and green flash on success, and a distinct “error” tone on mismatch【31†L115-L124】. Use *soft alerts* (in-page notifications) for scan errors so the user can immediately rescan without tapping “OK”【31†L92-L100】.

**Offline/sync states:** Always show connectivity status (e.g. a small icon or banner). Warn the user quietly if offline (e.g. “Offline – syncing locally”) and sync when back online. Retries should happen in background; don’t block the user. (This aligns with [29] advice to work offline gracefully【29†L621-L624】.)  

**Screen examples:** The **Receiving screen** might have a list of appointments (cards with dock # and carrier). Tapping one opens a shipment view: large “Scan Pallet” and “Add Damage” buttons. The **Scan Pallet** view opens the camera overlay with a marked target area. Once scanned, the UI shows a confirmation (e.g. a green overlay). **Adding damage** opens a mini inspector where user snaps a photo, tags the type, and can type a quick note.  

For **picking**, show one item per screen: e.g. “5 × [SKU] at A10”. Buttons: “Scan Location” (then “Scan Item”) – large green circles. After each pick, auto-advance to next. Keep a running total on screen. If an error, show a brief toast (“Expected 10, scanned 1, continue scanning”) rather than a blocking alert.  

**States & Accessibility:** Use high-contrast colors for statuses (green for done, yellow for warning, red for error). Make tap targets ≥44pt. Support VoiceOver by labeling buttons (e.g. “Scan Pallet button”).  

**Offline mode UI:** On loss of connection, show a banner (“Offline – saving locally”). Allow scans to queue and show a sync icon when reconnected.  

**Imagery:** In the final report we’d include wireframe mockups (or HTML previews) for key flows (Receive -> Scan, Pick -> Scan, Loading screen, etc.). For example, an annotated screenshot with overlay boxes for pallets or a mock HTML page showing the scanning UI. (Given length, we embed one representative screenshot here.)

## 8. Roles and permissions  
A simple RBAC with RLS on Supabase:

- **Worker (picker/receiver):** Can view *only their organization’s* data. Permissions: scan/modify shipments, inventory, tasks. RLS allows **insert/update** on items tied to their `org_id` or `user_id`. (e.g. a worker can only update `inventory_movements` if they initiated them.) They **cannot** change facility configs or see other orgs.  
- **Clerk/Dock Operator:** All worker rights plus ability to resolve exceptions, start/shutdown inspection, and mark shipments complete.  
- **Supervisor:** All clerk rights + appointment creation, order release, location master data, and user management (create/disable users).  
- **Admin/Owner:** All rights including billing configuration, API keys, role definitions.  
- **Driver (external):** If needed, can scan at check-in and make payment; only sees the single shipment they're in. (Likely out-of-scope for MVP RLS; interface through POS agent or simple web.)  

Use Supabase RLS with `auth.uid()` claims: e.g. `org_id = claim.organisation` or match on `user_id`. JWT from Clerk/Firebase can carry `org_id` and `role` claims. (“roles” table can define fine-grained JSON perms if needed【18†L33-L41】.)

## 9. API route map  
Design RESTful JSON endpoints; prefix with `/api`. We show core groups:

- **Auth:**  
  - `POST /api/auth/login` (returns JWT)  
  - `GET /api/auth/me` (user context)

- **Facilities/Docks:**  
  - `GET /api/facilities`, `POST`, `PATCH`, `DELETE` (Admin only)  
  - `GET /api/facilities/{id}/docks`

- **Appointments:** (Inbound)  
  - `GET /api/appointments?status=scheduled`  
  - `POST /api/appointments` (Admin sets schedule)  
  - `PATCH /api/appointments/{id}` (update status to arrived/completed)  

- **Inbound shipments & lines:**  
  - `POST /api/inbound_shipments` (start receiving)  
  - `GET /api/inbound_shipments` (list for user)  
  - `GET /api/inbound_shipments/{id}` (details)  
  - `PATCH /api/inbound_shipments/{id}` (e.g. change status to complete)  
  - `POST /api/inbound_lines` (add PO lines)  
  - `PATCH /api/inbound_lines/{id}` (update received_qty, status)  

- **Inventory/Locations:**  
  - `GET /api/inventory?sku=XYZ`  
  - `POST /api/inventory_movements` (log a move)  
  - `GET /api/locations`  

- **Tasks (receiving, picking, putaway):** (we might combine into shipments and orders)  
  - optional separate `/api/tasks/receive`, `/tasks/pick`… could be realized by status changes instead.

- **Outbound orders & lines:**  
  - `POST /api/outbound_orders`  
  - `GET /api/outbound_orders/{id}`, `PATCH /api/outbound_orders/{id}` (update status)  
  - `POST /api/outbound_lines`  
  - `PATCH /api/outbound_lines/{id}` (update qty_picked)  

- **Shipping:**  
  - `POST /api/outbound_orders/{id}/stage` (assign dock)  
  - `POST /api/outbound_orders/{id}/confirm` (mark shipped, capture signature/photo)  
  - `GET /api/outbound_orders/{id}/label` (retrieve BOL/label PDF)  

- **Cycle counts:**  
  - `GET /api/cycle_counts`  
  - `POST /api/cycle_counts`  
  - `PATCH /api/cycle_counts/{id}` (status, results)  

- **Exceptions:**  
  - `POST /api/exceptions` (report damage/shortage)  
  - `GET /api/exceptions` / `PATCH` (resolve)  

- **Inspections:** (future)  
  - `POST /api/inspection_sessions` (start new)  
  - `POST /api/inspection_captures` (upload photo)  
  - `[Stubs] POST /api/analyze-damage`  
  - `[Stubs] POST /api/ocr-document`  
  - `[Stubs] POST /api/inspection/compile-report`  

- **Payments:** (stubbed)  
  - `GET /api/payment/providers`  
  - `POST /api/payment/transactions` (initiate dock fee via provider)  
  - `GET /api/payment/transactions`  
  - `POST /api/payment/webhook/{provider}` (to receive confirmation)  

For each route: use standard REST conventions (idempotent GET, PATCH; POST for create). Mark in docs which are offline-capable (e.g. scanning inventory can queue to `/api/inventory_movements` when reconnected). Keep request/response shapes lean: e.g. a `POST /pick` might accept `{location: "...", sku: "...", qty: 5}`.  Use HTTP 200/201 for success, 400/409 for mismatches (short pick), 5xx for errors.

## 10. iOS module map

- **Inbound Module:**

  - ViewModels: `AppointmentListVM`, `ShipmentVM`.  
- **Outbound Module:**  
  - `OrderListView`, `PickingView`, `StagingView`, `ShipmentCloseView`.  
  - VMs: `OrderVM`, `PickVM`.  
- **Inventory Module:**  
  - `InventoryListView` (search by SKU), `CycleCountView`.  
  - VMs: `InventoryVM`, `CycleCountVM`.  
- **Tasks/Exceptions:**  
  - `CycleCountTaskView`, `ExceptionListView`.  
  - VMs: `TaskVM`, `ExceptionVM`.

- **Supervisor/Admin:**  
  - (If iOS admin needed) `AdminDashboardView` for configuring docks and users. (Alternatively, keep config web-only.)

Each module has associated Swift files and test targets. For example, **Inbound/Receiving** group: `ReceivingView.swift`, `ReceivingViewModel.swift`, `ReceivingService.swift`, `ReceivingModel.swift`.

## 11. Backend module map

- **src/**

    - `inbound/` (handlers for receiving shipments and lines)

## 13. 3PL/small warehouse pain points (product answers)  
- **High cost/complexity:** Most WMS require expensive hardware or consulting. *DockWalk answer:* smartphone-only (no dedicated scanners needed), simple onboarding (scan setup), pay-as-you-go via App Store.  
- **Paper & spreadsheets:** Manual logs cause errors. *Answer:* Digital scan-and-save of every transaction, automated reports (reduces manual count errors).  
- **Inventory inaccuracy:** Drift and losses. *Answer:* Frequent, easy cycle counts and real-time inventory updates with barcode scans prevent loss (with alerts for discrepancies).  
- **Damages/unaccounted cost:** WMS often ignore dock-level records. *Answer:* Built-in photo evidence for every damage/loss during receiving or loading (and optional AI highlight), so disputes are documented【27†L117-L125】.  
- **Slow receiving/picking:** Complex UIs slow workers. *Answer:* Single-purpose screens on iOS with big buttons & scan focus, designed for gloves and motion【31†L69-L77】.  
- **Seasonal staff onboarding:** Hard to train on desktop WMS. *Answer:* Intuitive mobile app requires minimal training – just scan and tap (“numbers on menu” trick)【31†L140-L146】.  
- **Lack of customization:** Heavy WMS are rigid. *Answer:* Configurable on-the-fly: easily add a new barcode or adjust a location in-app without IT help.  
- **Dock payment headaches:** Drivers paying fees is logistically messy. *Answer:* Built-in multi-POS support means drivers pay conveniently (e.g. chip & PIN, fleet card) and receipts auto-logged, rather than paper IOUs or separate processes.  

By solving these pains, DockWalk stands out as the **practical WMS for the rest of us**, not another overpriced enterprise product.
